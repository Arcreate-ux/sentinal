JEE 2028 Master Protocol: System Architecture & Execution1. System PhilosopAhyThis system is designed to eliminate psychological friction, goal paralysis, and perfectionism. It operates on deterministic execution, data generation, and mathematical accountability.Sequential Execution (No Clocks): We do not schedule by timestamps (e.g., 14:00 to 16:00). Biological systems cannot sustain zero-variance schedules over two years. We execute strictly by sequence. If a task takes longer than expected, the sequence simply shifts. The timeline never "collapses."Fail Fast: Perfectionism is a stall tactic. Spending 45 minutes on a single unresolved question is an operational failure. We enforce strict temporal cutoffs (e.g., 5-8 minutes per question). If unresolved, we drop it, log the failure, and move on. Data generation (even zeros) is infinitely more valuable than an empty database.The 80% Capacity Rule: The theoretical daily maximum is 300 points. We target an absolute baseline of 240 points. The remaining 20% capacity acts as a biological shock absorber for delays, difficult derivations, and fatigue.Asynchronous Logging: The database is a diagnostic ledger, not a real-time supervisor. We execute on physical paper and log the raw data into Notion only once per day during the final operational block.2. The Algorithmic Engine: Cognitive YieldThe system does not track "hours studied." It measures computational output using the Cognitive Yield formula. This ensures that time-wasting is penalized and high-focus flow states are rewarded.The Core VariablesAttempted ($A$): Number of questions initiated.Correct ($C$): Number of questions solved accurately.Actual Time ($T$): Total time spent on the block in minutes.Base Multiplier ($t_q$): The expected time per question, weighted by subject and difficulty. (e.g., Physics Ex 2A $t_q = 4.5$ mins; Math Ex 3B $t_q = 18.0$ mins).The MathTarget Time ($T_{target}$): $A \cdot t_q$Accuracy ($Acc$): $C / A$Velocity: $T_{target} / T$ (Mathematically capped to prevent reckless rushing).Accuracy Penalty Exponent: $10 / t_q$Final Yield Formula:
$$Yield = \text{round} \left( 1.666 \cdot T_{target} \cdot Acc^{(10/t_q)} \cdot Velocity \right)$$How it Manipulates Behavior:Accuracy is Exponential: Dropping accuracy slightly results in a massive yield collapse. It forces precision.Velocity is Linear: Taking twice as long as the Target Time halves your score. It forces pacing.Empty Days = Zero: If you do not input volume ($A = 0$), your maximum possible score is zero.3. Daily Sequence ProtocolsThese are the static operational queues.
